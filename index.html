Why these default cost projections

All figures are Anaweza's all-in annual support per scholar (USD), editable above. Defaults and rationale:

LevelDefault $/yrWhy
Lower Primary (G1-3)500Public day-primary tuition is government-funded; cost is uniforms, books, supplies and a modest upkeep stipend.
Upper Primary (G4-6)550As lower primary, with slightly higher materials.
Junior Secondary day (G7-9)650JSS tuition is capitation-funded and JSS is overwhelmingly day (domiciled in primary schools); cost is uniforms, transport, materials, upkeep.
Junior Secondary boarding750JSS boarding is uncommon in Kenya, so this is a small premium that rarely applies.
Senior Secondary day (G10-12)700Day tuition is government-funded since 2026; cost is upkeep, exams, materials.
Senior Secondary boarding1,000Boarding is standard and the costly tier at senior level: the 2026 gazette puts a boarding bill near 75,800 KES (about $592), plus Anaweza upkeep and extras.

University tuition uses the verified 2025 cluster fees; postgraduate uses an editable placeholder. Calibration: applied to a college-heavy cohort these blend toward the ~$1,796 per child Anaweza actually spent in 2025. They cover the scholar's costs, not organization-wide overhead.

Postgraduate and specialty costs

Masters and PhD are not band-funded. HELB offers a competitive partial postgraduate scholarship (~200,000 KES Masters, ~450,000 KES PhD), not guaranteed. Master of Medicine (specialty, ~4 yr) tuition runs ~247,000 to 345,000 KES/yr (University of Nairobi) or ~211,000 KES/yr (Aga Khan). Registrars are often self- or county-sponsored.

Funding model and legal status

The new Student-Centred Funding Model replaced the Differentiated Unit Cost (DUC) model in 2023. The High Court declared the new model unconstitutional on 20 Dec 2024; the Court of Appeal stayed that ruling on 26 Mar 2025, so it operates pending appeal. If struck down, a government-placed undergraduate reverts to the old DUC fee of about 16,000 KES/yr, not full fees.

Glossary

Band: need level (1 most vulnerable to 5 least needy) set by the Means Testing Instrument; determines the funding split.
Capitation: per-student grant the government pays a school.
KUCCPS: the body that places students into government-sponsored university and TVET slots.
HELB: Higher Education Loans Board, provides student loans (tuition and maintenance).
Universities Fund: awards the government scholarships.
DUC: Differentiated Unit Cost, the pre-2023 funding model.
Employment Bridge: months of living support at graduation until the first paycheck.
Setup: one-time $500 to set up a scholar entering higher education.
Graduated stage: already-finished alumni Anaweza still supports for a set number of months in the first year.
